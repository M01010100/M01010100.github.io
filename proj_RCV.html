Ranked Choice Voting

Ranked Choice Voting is a voting system that allows voters to rank candidates in order of preference. This system is used in many countries around the world and in several US cities. The system is designed to ensure that the winning candidate has the broadest possible support among voters. In this project, we will explore how Ranked Choice Voting works and how it can be implemented in the United States.

How Ranked Choice Voting Works

Ranked Choice Voting is a preferential voting system in which voters rank candidates in order of preference. In a Ranked Choice Voting election, voters are asked to rank candidates in order of preference, with their first choice being their top preference, their second choice being their second preference, and so on. Voters can rank as many candidates as they like, and they are not required to rank all candidates on the ballot.

Once all the votes are cast, the votes are counted in rounds. In each round, the candidate with the fewest first-choice votes is eliminated, and their votes are redistributed to the remaining candidates based on the voters' second choices. This process continues until one candidate receives a majority of the votes and is declared the winner.

Benefits of Ranked Choice Voting

Ranked Choice Voting has several benefits over traditional plurality voting systems. One of the main benefits is that it ensures that the winning candidate has the broadest possible support among voters. In a traditional plurality voting system, a candidate can win with less than a majority of the votes, which can lead to a lack of legitimacy and trust in the electoral process. Ranked Choice Voting also encourages candidates to run positive campaigns and reach out to a broader range of voters, as they need to appeal to a majority of voters to win.
