About

I am a current Graduate Student at SUNY Polytechnic Institute studying Computer Science. My research interests focus on machine learning security, data privacy, and the cryptographic applications of machine learning. This interest began as an undergraduate, when these topics uniquely appealed to me in lectures. For my undergraduate capstone, I investigated privacy-preserving machine learning, with an emphasis on Fully Homomorphic Encryption. Now, I am exploring the cryptographic use of machine learning, specifically analyzing models for cryptanalysis of block ciphers and side-channel attacks.

I am currently a Graduate assistant to Prof. Chen-Fu Chiang. The classes assigned to me are CS100, Intro to Compute Seminar - Fall 2025, and CS477 Algorithms - Spring 2026
